Day 1

Maa Shailputri

The daughter of the mountains, Shailputri is the embodiment of the power of Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. She rides a bull and carries a trident and lotus.

Significance

Represents the beginning of life and nature's awakening. Worshipping her brings stability and strength.

Day 2

Maa Brahmacharini

The ascetic form of Goddess Parvati, she holds a rosary and water pot. Her name means "one who practices devout austerity."

Significance

Symbolizes penance, devotion, and self-discipline. Grants wisdom and prosperity to devotees.

Day 3

Maa Chandraghanta

Named after the half-moon on her forehead shaped like a bell, she rides a tiger and possesses ten hands with various weapons.

Significance

Represents bravery and courage. Removes obstacles and brings peace and serenity to life.

Day 4

Maa Kushmanda

The creator of the universe, she is believed to have created the world with her divine smile. She radiates energy like the sun.

Significance

Brings light, energy, and health. Worshipping her removes darkness and negativity from life.

Day 5

Maa Skandamata

The mother of Lord Kartikeya (Skanda), she is depicted with four arms, holding her son in one arm while seated on a lotus.

Significance

Symbolizes motherly love and compassion. Brings wisdom and grants salvation to devotees.

Day 6

Maa Katyayani

The warrior goddess who destroyed the demon Mahishasura, she is one of the most violent forms of Goddess Parvati.

Significance

Represents fierce power and victory over evil. Worshipped for courage and strength in battles.

Day 7

Maa Kalaratri

The fiercest form of Durga with a dark complexion, she destroys all demons, ghosts, and negative energies.

Significance

Removes fear and ignorance. Despite her fierce appearance, she protects devotees from evil.

Day 8

Maa Mahagauri

Extremely beautiful with a fair complexion, she represents purity and peace. She rides a white bull and wears white clothes.

Significance

Symbolizes peace, compassion, and calmness. Cleanses sins and brings inner peace to devotees.

Day 9

Maa Siddhidatri

The giver of supernatural powers (Siddhis), she sits on a lotus and is worshipped by gods, sages, and devotees seeking spiritual perfection.

Significance

Bestows wisdom and all types of Siddhis. Represents the completion of the Navratri journey.
